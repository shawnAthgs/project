Phishing Scam

How to spot phishing:

  1. Check grammar and spelling.
  2. Check the sender’s email address.
  3. Watch for urgent threats or pressure.

Fake Check Scam

1. Feel the edges – fake checks may have rough or perforated edges.

2. Verify the bank address and check number.

3. Look for spelling mistakes and verify the sender.

Emergency Scam

A scammer pretends to be a stranded friend or family member and urgently asks for money.

  1. Details are vague or don’t match reality.
  2. The message comes from an unfamiliar address.
  3. They pressure you to act immediately.

Romance Scam

  • Heavy flattery to build quick trust.
  • Fast, intense declarations of love.
  • Stories of hardship to gain sympathy.

Ransomware Scam

Your files are “encrypted” and the attacker demands Bitcoin payment.

  • Poor spelling and grammar.
  • Demands for cryptocurrency payment.
  • Threats of permanent data loss if you don’t pay quickly.

Social Media Account Hacking Scam

Scammers target Snapchat, Instagram, and TikTok by stealing login information.

  1. “Is this you in this video?” links.
  2. Fake login pages that steal passwords.
  3. Threats that your account will be deleted.

Gaming Scam (Robux, Skins, Free Items)

Scammers target gamers in Roblox, Fortnite, and Discord with fake offers.

  1. Fake “free Robux” or “free V-Bucks” sites.
  2. Fake trading bots or middleman scams.
  3. Requests to verify accounts on fake websites.

Verification Code Scam

Scammers trick users into giving away login codes to take over accounts.

  1. “I sent you a code by mistake—can you send it back?”
  2. Fake friend accounts asking for verification codes.
  3. Used to hijack Snapchat, Instagram, and email accounts.
